## 2. Valuation purpose and methodology stack

This pack uses nine complementary lenses. Early-stage software assets with limited verified ARR are best triangulated by **replacement cost**, **technology / IP contribution**, and **scenario-based revenue or ARR multiples**—not by a single EBITDA exit multiple as if the company were mature SaaS.

| Method | Primary use here | Weight in triangulation |
|--------|------------------|-------------------------|
| Revenue Multiple | Forward illustrative revenue | Medium (scenario-dependent) |
| ARR Multiple | Forward illustrative ARR | Medium–High once ARR is real |
| EBITDA Multiple | Maturity / profitability path | Low today; rises with scale |
| Rule of 40 | Operating quality screen | Qualitative until metrics exist |
| Comparables | Category context | Medium (with stage discount) |
| Strategic Premium | Acquirer synergy narrative | Deal-specific |
| Replacement Cost | Floor for build-vs-buy | High at this stage |
| Technology Value | Product / architecture asset | High |
| IP Value | Brand, legal, content, code | Medium–High |

**Triangulation rule used in this memo:**  
**Indicative enterprise value (EV) range ≈ blend of (Replacement Cost + Technology/IP) as a floor band, with (scenario ARR × multiple) as an upside band, adjusted for strategic premium only when a named-fit acquirer thesis is credible.**

## 4. Method 1 — Revenue Multiple

### Definition
EV ≈ **Trailing or forward revenue × industry multiple**.

### Application (honest)
Trailing revenue is **not claimed**. Method applied to **illustrative Year-1 / Year-2 revenue bands** from `Financial-Projections.md` and roadmap scenario bands.

### Assumptions

| Assumption | Conservative | Expected | Aggressive |
|------------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Forward annual revenue (illustrative) | $80k | $180k | $350k |
| Revenue multiple (early AI SaaS / pre-scale) | 2.0× | 4.0× | 7.0× |
| Justification | Weak MoR; slow conversion | Steady self-serve + light agency | Strong agency + Premium mix |

### Indicative EV (Revenue Multiple)

| Case | Math | Indicative EV |
|------|------|---------------|
| Conservative | $80k × 2.0 | **~$160k** |
| Expected | $180k × 4.0 | **~$720k** |
| Aggressive | $350k × 7.0 | **~$2.45M** |

**Caveat:** Multiples compress if churn is high, GPU COGS dominate, or category leaders price aggressively. Multiples expand if NRR > 120% and agency land-and-expand is proven.

## 7. Method 4 — Rule of 40

### Definition
**Growth % + Profit margin % ≥ 40** is a heuristic for “high-quality” SaaS; better Rule-of-40 scores historically correlate with higher multiples.

### Application (honest)
Without verified growth and margin, Rule of 40 is used as a **scoring screen for scenario quality**, not as a calculator of EV.

| Scenario quality | Illustrative growth | Illustrative margin | Rule of 40 score | Multiple implication |
|------------------|---------------------|---------------------|------------------|----------------------|
| Conservative | 40% | −20% | 20 | Discount to peer multiples |
| Expected | 80% | 0% | 80 | Near-peer early SaaS multiples |
| Aggressive | 120% | 10% | 130 | Premium to early SaaS multiples |

**Board takeaway:** Until growth and contribution margin are measured monthly, treat Rule of 40 as a **management KPI design**, not a valuation proof.

## 8. Method 5 — Comparables

### Category context (directional — not RTAS financials)

Public and private AI video / creative tools (Runway, Pika, Luma, HeyGen, Synthesia, and adjacent creative SaaS) span wide valuations driven by **funding round marks**, **ARR scale**, and **enterprise mix**. Early private marks are noisy and often not applicable to bootstrap or pre-seed assets.

### Stage discount framework applied here

| Peer-like narrative | Typical narrative multiple | Discount applied to RTAS at this stage | Why |
|---------------------|----------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----|
| Scaled AI video SaaS | High double-digit ARR multiples in hot markets | 70–90% | No verified ARR scale; brand awareness gap |
| Early creative SaaS | Mid-single-digit ARR multiples | 30–50% | MoR / GTM execution risk |
| Studio-packaged niche SaaS | 3–8× ARR when retention proven | 0–20% | Closest conceptual fit once ARR exists |

### Indicative EV from comps (ARR-linked)

Using Expected illustrative ARR $150k × (6× after stage discount from noisier category marks) → **~$900k**, consistent with Method 2 Expected case.

**Caveat:** Do not cite a competitor’s last funding valuation as RTAS’s value.

## 10. Method 7 — Replacement Cost

### Definition
What it would cost a competent team to **rebuild equivalent capability** (product, infra glue, legal/trust surface, GTM packaging)—not the cost of training foundation models.

| Brand, domain, content, SEO baseline | $10k | $25k | $45k |
