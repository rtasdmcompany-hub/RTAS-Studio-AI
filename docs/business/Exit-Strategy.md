## 1. Decision framework

| Criterion | Preference when true |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Need liquidity soon | Strategic acquisition or MBO/partial sale |
| Want to scale with capital, retain control longer | Growth equity |
| Want operational partner without full sale | Merger / joint platform integration |
| Maximize long-horizon public liquidity | IPO (distant) |
| Financial engineering / buyout structure | PE (more relevant post-ARR) |

**Gates before any serious process:** Paddle live · clean IP assignments · access matrix · honest metrics pack · VDR populated per `Virtual-Data-Room-Index.md`.

## 2. Strategic acquisition

**Definition:** Sale to a corporation that values product, team, customers, or geographic/compliance fit (see `Acquirer-Target-List.md`).

### Pros
- Highest probability of **synergy premium** vs pure financial buyers at this stage.  
- Faster path to distribution (acquirer channels).  
- Validates product packaging and trust narrative.  
- Can be structured as asset sale or equity sale to fit operator entity realities.

### Cons
- Integration risk (product sunset or brand absorption).  
- Earn-outs may tie value to post-close metrics still unproven.  
- Cultural / geographic diligence friction.  
- Acquirer leverage high while ARR is thin.

### Fit to RTAS today
**Primary near-term exit thesis.** Best matched to creative AI platforms, martech, regional digital groups, or media toolchains needing a studio-shaped AI video offering.

### Readiness
Partial — product/docs strong; MoR + corporate pack incomplete.

---

## 3. Private equity (PE)

**Definition:** Control or significant stake by a PE fund, often with leverage and operational value-creation plan.

### Pros
- Capital for GTM and hiring.  
- Professionalization of finance/ops.  
- Potential secondary liquidity for founder over time.

### Cons
- Early-stage ARR likely **below typical PE buyout thresholds**.  
- Governance intensity and reporting burden.  
- Debt structures inappropriate before stable cash flows.  
- May push growth over compliance if poorly aligned.

### Fit to RTAS today
**Low near-term.** More plausible after Expected/Aggressive ARR trajectories in `Financial-Projections.md` are evidenced (hundreds of thousands to millions ARR) and margins stabilize.

### Readiness
Missing as a live option until traction and audited/management accounts exist.

---

## 4. Growth equity

**Definition:** Minority (sometimes significant) investment to fund growth without full founder exit.

### Pros
- Preserves upside.  
- Brings network and board discipline.  
- Aligns with “optional seed” language in business roadmap.  
- Less disruptive than full sale.

### Cons
- Dilution.  
- Requires credible metrics story (even if early).  
- Investor may still demand MoR proof and unit economics.  
- Not an “exit” for full liquidity—partial at best.

### Fit to RTAS today
**Secondary path** if MoR is live and early retention is real; otherwise premature.

### Readiness
Partial (narrative ready; metrics pending).

---

## 5. Merger

**Definition:** Combination with a complementary company (agency group, creative tools startup, regional SaaS) into a shared entity.

### Pros
- Can solve distribution or product gaps bilaterally.  
- May unlock regional strength (Pakistan / MENA / South Asia) plus global English product.  
- Shared cost base for inference and GTM.

### Cons
- Complex governance and valuation of relative contributions.  
- Integration failure common in peer mergers.  
- Brand confusion risk.  
- Harder to unwind than a clean acquisition.

### Fit to RTAS today
**Opportunistic.** Consider if a complementary operator wants AI studio capability and brings customers under AUP-compatible use cases.

### Readiness
Partial — strategy docs exist; no active merger process asserted.

---

## 6. Management buyout (MBO) / operator buy-up

**Definition:** Management/operator consolidates ownership (or buys out partners/advisors) using cash, seller notes, or structured finance.

### Pros
- Cleaner control for long-term build.  
- Useful if external bids undervalue replacement-cost floor.  
- Keeps mission and compliance posture intact.

### Cons
- Limited liquidity for selling parties.  
- Financing hard without cash flows.  
- Does not solve GTM capital needs by itself.

### Fit to RTAS today
**Relevant for internal capitalization cleanup** before external process; not a liquidity event for the operator if already controlling.

### Readiness
Depends on current ownership paperwork (OWNER UPLOAD).

---

## 7. IPO

**Definition:** Public listing.

### Pros
- Maximum theoretical liquidity and brand prestige.  
- Currency for acquisitions.

### Cons
- **Not realistic** at current scale.  
- Regulatory cost, disclosure burden, AI scrutiny.  
- Category leaders still define investor mindshare.

### Fit to RTAS today
**Long-term optionality only** (multi-year, post substantial ARR, governance, and compliance maturity). Do not use IPO language in near-term buyer materials.

### Readiness
Missing / N/A near term.

  Reassess PE / larger strategic / (only then) public-market fantasy

## 10. Deal structure notes (early-stage)

| Structure | When useful |
|-----------|-------------|
| Asset purchase of product/IP/domain | Entity complexity or clean carve-out |
| Equity purchase of operating company | Clean cap table & IP inside entity |
| Earn-out on MRR / gross profit | Bridges thin trailing metrics |
| Talent / consulting agreements | Key-person retention post-close |
| Escrow for indemnity | IP, compliance, tax unknowns |

## 11. What destroys exit value

1. Invented traction in teasers.  
2. AUP regression (deepfake marketing).  
3. Uncleared IP ownership.  
4. Single-console access only founder holds.  
5. MoR still blocked at signing.  
6. Customer concentration (when customers exist) undisclosed.
