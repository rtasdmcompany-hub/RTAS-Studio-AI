## 1. Fundraising posture (honest)

RTAS Studio AI should treat fundraising as **optional acceleration**, not oxygen, until MoR collection and early retention evidence exist. Sprint 1 investor readiness scored as foundational narrative with missing deck/data room artifacts—outreach must not outrun materials.

**Always true in outreach**

- Verified pricing and product URL only  
- No invented ARR, MAU, valuation, or customer logos  
- Paddle MoR status disclosed accurately (including pending checkout gaps)  
- Enterprise described as design-partner trajectory, not installed base  
- No deepfake positioning  

**Raise when:** checkout works, ICP conversion is observable, and a crisp use-of-proceeds story exists.  
**Do not raise into:** broken payments, undefined unit economics, or hype-only narratives.

## 2. Investor segments

### 2.1 Venture capital (pre-seed / seed)

| Fit | Why |
|-----|-----|
| Funds with thesis in generative AI, creator tools, martech, or emerging-market SaaS with global distribution | Category adjacency |
| Partners who accept early-stage packaging honesty | Avoids diligence whiplash |

**Avoid for now:** Growth funds needing proven ARR scale; deepfake-tolerant consumer gimmick theses.

**Ask shape:** Pre-seed/seed exploration sized to GTM + compute + selective enterprise packaging—not a fake Series A story.

### 2.2 Angels

| Fit | Why |
|-----|-----|
| Operators from agencies, media, SaaS GTM, AI product | Practical help + capital |
| Diaspora / Pakistan–MENA–US angels who understand cross-border SaaS | Network leverage |
| Prior creators-tool investors | Empathy for credit-metered products |

**Angel value beyond cash:** intros to agencies, hiring, MoR/ops pattern recognition.

### 2.3 Strategic investors (corporates, agencies holding groups)

| Fit | Why |
|-----|-----|
| Marketing groups seeking AI production capability | Distribution |
| Media/tech firms needing studio-adjacent tools | Product synergy |

**Caution:** Strategics can slow process and demand exclusivity. Prefer non-exclusive pilots before equity talks.

### 2.4 Corporate venture (CVC)

| Fit | Why |
|-----|-----|
| Cloud, creative-software, ad-tech, payments-adjacent CVCs | Infrastructure alignment |

**Caution:** Strategic rights, information rights, and long cycle times. Use CVC when product synergy is real—not as vanity.

## 3. Warm outreach (primary path)

Warm > cold for early-stage trust.

### 3.1 Warm source map

| Source | Motion |
|--------|--------|
| RTAS Digital Marketing Company clients & partners | Ask for investor intros only after value delivered |
| Founder personal network | Rank by relevance to AI/SaaS/media |
| Existing angels’ peer intros | Double-opt-in introductions |
| Accelerator/community alumni (if any) | Thesis-matched intros |
| Customers (once paying) | “Investor who should see this” asks—never pressure |

### 3.2 Warm intro request template (to mutual connection)

> Could you introduce me to [Investor] for a brief look at RTAS Studio AI—an AI video studio (rtasstudio.com) with seconds-based credits and a trust-first authorized Identity Preservation posture? We’re [bootstrapped / exploring a seed conversation] with live product and published pricing; happy to keep claims limited to verified traction. Here’s a one-paragraph blurb you can forward: […]

Provide a **forwardable blurb** (5–7 lines) every time—do the connector’s work.

### 3.3 Warm first meeting goal

Not to close a round on call one. Goal: **permission for a second meeting** + clear diligence questions list.

## 4. Cold outreach (secondary path)

Cold is acceptable at low volume with extreme personalization.

### 4.1 Targeting rules

- Thesis match evidenced by recent investments (generative media, creator SaaS, martech)  
- Geography open to Pakistan-operated / global digital companies  
- Stage match (pre-seed/seed)  
- No spray of 500 identical emails  

### 4.2 Cold email structure (≤120 words)

1. Why them (one specific portfolio or thesis proof)  
2. What RTAS is (studio categories + credit math)  
3. Trust wedge (authorized IP, legal v1.1, Paddle MoR)  
4. Stage honesty + ask (15-min call)  
5. Link: https://rtasstudio.com  

**Do not** attach fake traction decks. If deck exists later, send only facts-labeled materials from `business/investors/`.

### 4.3 Cold LinkedIn

Same content compressed; no attachment spam; one bump max after 5–7 days.

## 5. Outreach schedule (operating rhythm)

### 5.1 Pre-outreach gate (complete first)

| Gate | Status check |
|------|--------------|
| Product live | https://rtasstudio.com |
| Pricing consistent | Tester / Standard / Premium |
| Legal suite live | v1.1 trust pages |
| MoR narrative accurate | Paddle + known gaps |
| Company + SWOT + roadmap readable | Sprint 1 pack |
| One-pager / deck | Build before scaling outreach (currently reserved under `business/investors/`) |

### 5.2 Weekly cadence (when actively fundraising)

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Mon | Pipeline review; prioritize 10 targets |
| Tue–Wed | Warm intro asks + 5 personalized cold notes |
| Thu | Follow-ups; send diligence answers |
| Fri | Update CRM/investor tracker; refine narrative from objections |

**Volume target (lean):** 8–15 quality conversations/month > 100 empty emails.

### 5.3 Meeting ladder

1. **Intro (15–20 min)** — problem, product, wedge, ask  
2. **Deep dive (45 min)** — GTM, ICP, competition honesty, MoR, roadmap  
3. **Diligence** — data room facts only  
4. **Partner meeting / angel close**  
5. **Docs**

## 6. Follow-up system

| Touch | Timing | Content |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Thank-you | Same day | 5-bullet recap + links to live pages |
| Soft bump | Day 5 | Answer one anticipated diligence question |
| Value bump | Day 12 | Verified product/MoR update—not vanity metrics |
| Break-up | Day 21 | Graceful close; leave door open |
| Quarterly nurture | If soft no | One factual progress note |

**Tracker fields:** Investor · Firm · Type (VC/Angel/Strategic/CVC) · Source · Stage · Last touch · Next step · Thesis fit (1–5) · Notes · Pass reason  

Log in the same CRM or a dedicated investor pipeline—never only in chat history.

3. **Wedge:** Trust-first, authorized Identity Preservation, MoR professionalism

**SWOT honesty:** Use `business/company/SWOT.md`; early-stage risks are features of credibility, not weaknesses to hide.
