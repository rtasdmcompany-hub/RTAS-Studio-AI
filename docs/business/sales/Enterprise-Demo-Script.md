| Checklist | Done |
|-----------|------|
| Confirm attendees + roles (champion, economic buyer, legal/security if present) | ☐ |
| Re-read discovery notes; pick **one** customer brief to mirror | ☐ |
| Authorized sample assets ready (no celebrity/unauthorized likeness) | ☐ |
| Logged into https://rtasstudio.com; credits available | ☐ |
| Pricing table memorized: Tester $5/30s/5d · Standard $89/2000s · Premium $249/2000s · 1 credit = 1s | ☐ |
| Legal URLs open: Terms, Privacy, AI Policy, Trust & Safety | ☐ |
| MoR/checkout status known (state facts only) | ☐ |
| CRM opportunity stage = Demo; next-step field ready | ☐ |

**Demo promise:** One clear path from their brief → generation concept → credit math → trust posture → written next step.

---

## Minute 0–3 — Open and frame

**Say:**

> Thanks for joining. I’m [Name] with RTAS Studio AI—operated by RTAS Digital Marketing Company. In the next 30 minutes we’ll confirm your use case, show how the studio handles it, walk the seconds-based pricing, and cover trust/authorized-use posture. We’ll end with a concrete pilot or plan recommendation—not a vague maybe.

**Ask:**

> What does a win look like for you personally from today’s call?

Capture answer aloud; restate in one sentence.

---

## Minute 3–8 — Discovery refresh (tight)

Ask only what is missing:

1. Which video types matter in the next 60 days—ads, music visuals, animation, social packs?  
2. Rough monthly volume—deliverables or seconds?  
3. Who else must say yes—brand, legal, procurement, finance?  
4. Any likeness or talent-rights constraints?  
5. Are you open to a 30–90 day pilot if the workflow fits?

**Gate:** If they want unauthorized impersonation tools, **stop the sales demo**, explain Trust & Safety position, and decline politely.

---

## Minute 8–18 — Live product narrative (core demo)

### 8.1 Position (60–90 seconds)

> RTAS Studio AI is an AI video studio for commercials, music videos, animation, and brand storytelling. We meter usage in seconds—one credit equals one second—so teams can budget like production, not like mystery tokens. Identity Preservation is for authorized media only; we do not market deepfakes or face-swap misuse.

### 8.2 Show the workflow (6–7 minutes)

Walk **their** brief shape:

1. Start from text and/or image input aligned to their campaign  
2. Explain category fit (commercial / MV / animation / social)  
3. Trigger or show a representative generation path  
4. Call out long-form behavior if relevant: longer Standard/Premium jobs split into ~15s segments and auto-stitch  
5. Show where credits will decrement conceptually  
6. If Identity Preservation is in scope: emphasize **authorization** and rights ownership  

**Talk track while rendering / waiting:**

> While this runs—quality depends on inputs and capacity, which we disclose in Terms. Your evaluation should judge fit for *your* channel standards, not every cinematic genre at once.

### 8.3 Contrast without competitor-bashing (60 seconds)

> Avatar L&D tools optimize presenters. Frontier labs optimize model research. We’re oriented to studio production outcomes with commercial packaging and a trust-first AUP story—especially for agencies and marketing teams who need client-safe language.

---

## Minute 18–22 — Pricing and packaging

**Whiteboard / screen share the ladder:**

| Plan | Price | Seconds | When to choose |
|------|-------|---------|----------------|
| Tester | $5 | 30s / 5 days | Prove quality cheaply |
| Standard | $89/mo | 2000s | HD commercial workhorse |
| Premium 4K | $249/mo | 2000s | Cinematic 4K / priority IP queuing |

**Math moment:**

> If you need roughly [X] seconds a month across seats, you’re in [Standard / multi-seat Standard / Premium] territory. Pilot designs usually start at Premium or a small seat cluster—not a fake unlimited enterprise SKU.

**MoR:**

> Checkout is via Paddle as Merchant of Record. [Insert accurate status: live / pending domain approval / known limitation.] We will not pretend collection works if it doesn’t.

Handle discount pressure: prefer onboarding help over deep cuts; escalate >15% to founder policy.

---

## Minute 22–26 — Trust and enterprise diligence

Cover crisply:

- Live legal suite including AI Policy and Trust & Safety **v1.1**  
- Authorized Identity Preservation only  
- Refund policy exists  
- Support: support@rtasstudio.com  
- Honest gaps: SSO, SOC 2, formal uptime SLA—**roadmap / not claimed today**  
- Pilot is how serious buyers de-risk early-stage vendors  

Invite questions; answer with facts or “I’ll confirm in writing”—never invent certifications.

---

## Minute 26–29 — Recommendation and close

**Recommend one path:**

A. **Self-serve:** Tester today → Standard within the week  
B. **Team:** N× Standard seats  
C. **Pilot:** 30/60/90-day Premium or multi-seat package → send proposal within [2 business days]

**Ask for the close:**

> Are you comfortable moving to [A/B/C]? If yes, who signs and what is the procurement path?

Book the next meeting **on the call**. Confirm email for proposal.

---

## Minute 29–30 — Recap and CRM

Recap in 3 bullets: use case, recommended plan, next step + date.  
Thank them. End on time.

**After hang-up (5 minutes):**

- Update CRM stage, notes, score, next step  
- Send summary email within 2 hours  
- If proposal path: draft from [`Enterprise-Proposal-Template.md`](./Enterprise-Proposal-Template.md)

## Objection pocket cards

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| “Show me deepfake of a celebrity” | We don’t. Authorized use only—non-negotiable. |
| “We need SOC 2 this quarter” | Not claiming it today. Pilot under current controls + policies, or revisit when roadmap lands. |
| “Runway already used here” | Complementary evaluation on packaging, credits, trust narrative, and your specific commercial workflows. |
| “Too early” | Agreed we’re early—that’s why Tester/pilot exists. Paddle MoR + legal suite are deliberate foundations. |
| “Price vs free tools” | Free tools rarely survive client brand review or predictable seconds budgeting. |
| “Checkout failed” | Execution gap—we’ll resolve MoR status and follow up with a working path. |
