## 1. GTM thesis

Win English-first creators, agencies, and marketing companies with a **trust-first AI video studio**, transparent seconds-based pricing, and a low-friction Tester entry—then graduate fit accounts into Standard ($89/mo) and Premium 4K ($249/mo), while running selective **design-partner enterprise pilots**.

**Category:** AI video studio / generative video SaaS  
**Primary wedge:** Commercial studio use cases + credit clarity + authorized Identity Preservation + live legal/trust suite + Paddle MoR path  
**Anti-wedge:** Deepfake marketing, unauthorized likeness, “unlimited free cinema,” fake enterprise maturity

**Critical gate:** Paddle checkout / domain approval must be production-stable before scaling paid acquisition. Until then, GTM spend stays founder-led and organic-heavy. *(Execution gap — track weekly.)*

## 2. Launch strategy

### 2.1 Launch definition (commercial)

Launch is not “website exists.” Commercial launch means:

1. Product live at https://rtasstudio.com  
2. Published pricing ladder enforceable in checkout  
3. Legal pages live (Terms, Privacy, Refund, Cookies, AI Policy, Trust & Safety v1.1)  
4. Support path staffed (support@rtasstudio.com)  
5. MoR collecting successfully on target plans  

### 2.2 Phased launch waves

| Wave | Audience | Offer | Channel mix | Goal |
|------|----------|-------|-------------|------|
| **Wave A — Soft commercial** | Warm network, RTAS DM clients, creator friends | Tester → Standard | Direct email, WhatsApp/LinkedIn, founder demos | Prove payment + first paid seats |
| **Wave B — Self-serve public** | English-web creators & freelancers | Tester featured; Standard primary CTA | SEO, site conversion, content | Repeatable signup→paid |
| **Wave C — Agency wedge** | Agencies & marketing companies | Multi-seat Standard; Premium for hero work | Outbound + partnerships | Design partners & case permission |
| **Wave D — Enterprise pilots** | Mid-market marketing orgs | 30–90 day pilot SOW | Founder enterprise motion | Diligence learning + ACV path |

Do not run Wave D paid media until Waves A–B conversion math is known.

### 2.3 Messaging for launch

- **Creators:** Clear seconds pricing; Tester $5 proof  
- **Agencies:** More concepts per sprint; client-safe trust narrative  
- **Enterprise evaluators:** Policy clarity and MoR professionalism—honest early-stage packaging

## 4. Pricing rollout

| Plan | Price | Capacity | GTM role |
|------|-------|----------|----------|
| Tester | $5 · 30 seconds · 5 days | Evaluation | Acquisition wedge; proof of quality |
| Standard | $89/mo · 2000 seconds | HD commercial path | Primary revenue plan |
| Premium 4K | $249/mo · 2000 seconds | Cinematic 4K | ARPU expansion; enterprise pilots often start here |
| Credit unit | 1 credit = 1 second | — | Universal sales math |

### 4.1 Rollout rules

1. **Publish once, sell consistently** — all regions see the same public ladder unless MoR forces currency display differences  
2. **No shadow SKUs** in early GTM — custom enterprise = multi-seat / pilot term on top of published economics  
3. **Annual prepay (optional later)** — only after monthly retention evidence; not required for launch  
4. **Coupons** — time-boxed Tester boosts or first-month agency trials only with founder approval  
5. **Never** imply Tester economics produce unlimited 4K commercial masters  

### 4.2 Packaging gaps (honest)

Multi-seat admin, client workspaces, SSO, formal SLA — treat as roadmap packaging. Sell pilots with clear scope rather than fake enterprise editions.

## 7. CAC strategy

### 7.1 Philosophy

At this stage, **capital-efficient learning** beats vanity growth. Optimize for **payback on Standard/Premium**, not raw signups.

### 7.2 Channel CAC posture (planning)

| Channel | Expected CAC character | Notes |
|---------|------------------------|-------|
| Organic / SEO | Low cash CAC; high time cost | Primary until MoR proven |
| Founder outbound | Low cash; high founder time | Best for agency ACV |
| Partnerships | Variable; often low cash | Track assisted revenue |
| Paid social / search | Higher cash CAC | Unlock after conversion baseline |
| Events | High per-lead cash | Use for pipeline quality, not volume |

### 7.3 Unit-economics anchors (from published pricing)

Illustrative planning only—not verified historical LTV:

- Standard: **$89/mo** → if retained 6 months ≈ **$534** gross bookings before refunds/cost of goods  
- Premium: **$249/mo** → 6 months ≈ **$1,494**  
- Blended early target: keep **fully loaded CAC** well below expected 6-month gross for the acquired mix  

Formal formulas: [`Sales-KPI.md`](./Sales-KPI.md)  
Finance depth: reserved under `business/finance/` (expand in later sprints)

### 7.4 CAC kill switches

Pause or cut a channel if:

- Checkout failure rate blocks conversion  
- Lead→paid < threshold after 2 test cycles  
- Support load from low-intent traffic exceeds capacity  
- Creative attracts unauthorized-use inquiries (quality of intent failure)

## 8. Growth strategy

1. **Tester → Standard** activation loop (product-led)  
2. **Standard → Premium** quality/ARPU loop  
3. **Single seat → agency pod** expansion loop  
4. **Design partner → referral / case** credibility loop

### 8.2 2026 growth priorities (aligned to business roadmap)

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 1 | Stabilize MoR collection |
| 2 | Measure Tester→Standard conversion |
| 3 | Run agency outbound with ICP language |
| 4 | Collect 3–5 permissioned narratives (not fabricated metrics) |
| 5 | Open enterprise pilots only with clear SOW |

Scenario revenue bands remain **planning assumptions** in `business/roadmap/BUSINESS_ROADMAP.md` (illustrative Low/Base/High)—not forecasts for public decks.

### 8.3 What “good growth” looks like

- Paying accounts of **high ICP fit**  
- Predictable credit utilization without chronic support chaos  
- Zero tolerance for deepfake positioning drift  
- Pipeline visibility via CRM stages  
- Renewals and expansions contributing to MRR before heavy paid spend
