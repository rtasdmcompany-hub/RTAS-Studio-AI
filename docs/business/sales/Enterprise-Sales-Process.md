## Purpose

Define the end-to-end enterprise motion from **Lead → Renewal** for mid-market and enterprise marketing organizations evaluating RTAS Studio AI as a **governed generative video capability**.

**Honesty gate:** Enterprise is a developing ICP. This process sells **pilots and design partnerships**, not a fictional global deployment history. Do not claim Fortune-500 references unless verified.

**Execution gap:** Paddle MoR checkout may still be pending domain/approval completion. Enterprise closes that require card/invoice collection must sync with real MoR status.

## Stage machine (Lead → Renewal)

| # | Stage | Exit criteria | Primary owner | Typical duration |
|---|-------|---------------|---------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Lead** | Identified account + contact + trigger | Founder / AE | 0–3 days |
| 2 | **Qualify** | ICP fit + authorized-use intent + BANT-lite ≥6 | Founder / AE | 3–7 days |
| 3 | **Discovery** | Jobs, volume, stakeholders, success metrics documented | Founder / AE | 1–2 weeks |
| 4 | **Demo** | Live or guided session completed; stakeholder feedback captured | Founder / AE | 1 week from discovery |
| 5 | **Technical / Trust review** | Legal/security questions mapped to live policies; gaps logged honestly | Founder + policy owner | 1–3 weeks |
| 6 | **Proposal** | Written pilot/commercial proposal delivered | Founder / AE | 3–7 days |
| 7 | **Negotiation** | Commercial + legal redlines converging | Founder | 1–4 weeks |
| 8 | **Closed-Won** | Contract/order + payment path confirmed | Founder | Event |
| 9 | **Onboarding** | Kickoff done; first successful governed outputs | CS / founder | 1–2 weeks |
| 10 | **Adoption** | Usage against pilot success criteria | CS | Pilot period |
| 11 | **Expansion** | Seats, Premium mix, or wider teams | AE / founder | Ongoing |
| 12 | **Renewal** | Renewal decision + terms for next term | AE / founder | T−60 to T−0 |

**Closed-Lost** can occur from Qualify onward—require a loss reason code in CRM.

---

## Stage 1 — Lead

**Definition:** A named organization with a plausible video/marketing need and at least one contact.

**Entry sources:** Inbound contact@ / info@; outbound agency lists; partner intro; conference; investor/strategic warm intro.

**Actions**

- Create CRM record (account + contact)  
- Tag source and segment (Enterprise Marketing / Agency-Enterprise hybrid)  
- First response ≤4 business hours for inbound  

**Exit:** Lead accepted into Qualify or discarded (disqualified).

---

## Stage 2 — Qualify

**Must-pass filters**

| Filter | Pass | Fail |
|--------|------|------|
| Use case | Commercials, campaign video, animation, MV, brand films | Unauthorized deepfake / impersonation |
| Buyer type | Marketing, brand, creative ops, innovation | Pure research tire-kicker with no owner |
| Mindset | Willing to pilot | Demands full SSO/SOC2/MSA day-one with no evaluation path |
| Commercial | Can fund Premium or multi-seat Standard pilot band | Expects free unlimited enterprise |

**Spend planning band (estimate only):** Pilot ~$249–$2,000+/mo equivalent; true enterprise ACV TBD until evidence exists (`business/sales/ICP.md`).

**Exit:** Qualified Opportunity or Disqualified (reason logged).

---

## Stage 3 — Discovery

Run the discovery framework in [`Sales-Playbook.md`](./Sales-Playbook.md). Enterprise-specific adds:

- Brand safety and talent-rights workflow  
- Procurement path (PO, vendor form, security questionnaire)  
- Data handling expectations vs. current SaaS reality  
- Success metrics for a 30/60/90-day pilot  
- Internal champions vs. economic buyers  

**Artifact:** Discovery brief (1 page) attached to CRM.

**Exit:** Demo scheduled with the right stakeholders (champion + ideally economic buyer or influencer).

---

## Stage 4 — Demo

Execute [`Enterprise-Demo-Script.md`](./Enterprise-Demo-Script.md).

**Enterprise demo rules**

- Authorized sample assets only  
- Explicit Identity Preservation framing  
- Credit math on a whiteboard moment (seconds → dollars)  
- Live legal URLs ready  
- MoR/checkout status stated accurately  

**Exit:** Stakeholder interest confirmed → Technical/Trust review or straight to Proposal for simple pilots.

---

## Stage 5 — Technical / Trust review

**Provide (verified assets only)**

- Terms, Privacy, Refund, Cookies  
- AI Policy, Trust & Safety v1.1  
- Paddle MoR explanation  
- Authorized Identity Preservation positioning  
- Support contacts  

**Do not fabricate**

- SOC 2 / ISO certificates you do not hold  
- Uptime SLAs not published  
- Customer logos without permission  
- On-prem or air-gapped deployment  

**Exit:** Buyer risk accepted for pilot **or** parked until roadmap items land **or** Closed-Lost.

---

## Stage 6 — Proposal

Use [`Enterprise-Proposal-Template.md`](./Enterprise-Proposal-Template.md).

Minimum commercial contents:

- Pilot scope and use cases  
- Seats / plans (Standard / Premium) and credit pools  
- Term length (30/60/90 days typical)  
- Price and payment via Paddle (or agreed invoice path if/when available)  
- Success criteria and review date  
- AUP / authorized-use acknowledgment  
- Explicit exclusions (no unauthorized likeness; no deepfake workflows)

**Exit:** Proposal under review → Negotiation.

---

## Stage 7 — Negotiation

**Flexible:** seat count, pilot length, onboarding sessions, reporting cadence  
**Constrained:** public list economics as the floor; AUP language; unauthorized-use bans  
**Escalation:** any custom indemnity, unlimited liability, or security commitment beyond fact → founder + counsel  

**Exit:** Verbal agreement → contract/order execution path.

---

## Stage 8 — Closed-Won

Checklist:

- [ ] Commercial terms accepted  
- [ ] Payment method live (Paddle) or documented alternative  
- [ ] Named admin contacts collected  
- [ ] Kickoff date set  
- [ ] CRM stage Closed-Won + ARR/MRR fields updated (pilot MRR recognized per finance policy)  
- [ ] Slack/email internal win note (facts only)

---

## Stage 9 — Onboarding

| Day | Milestone |
|-----|-----------|
| 0 | Welcome, access, plan confirmation |
| 1–3 | Workflow training; credit budgeting |
| 7 | First campaign-style outputs reviewed |
| 14 | Mid-pilot check vs. success criteria |
| End−7 | Expansion / renewal conversation scheduled |

---

## Stage 10 — Adoption

Track leading indicators:

- Active generators / week  
- Seconds consumed vs. plan  
- Support ticket severity  
- Stakeholder sentiment  
- Policy incidents (target: zero unauthorized-use attempts progressing)

If adoption lags: intervene with office hours, prompt libraries, or narrower use-case focus—not silent neglect.

---

## Stage 11 — Expansion

Triggers:

- Additional brand teams want access  
- Need for Premium 4K hero work  
- Agency-of-record wants nested seats  
- Geographic team rollout  

Expansion still respects packaging honesty (no fake SSO upsell).

---

## Stage 12 — Renewal

| Window | Action |
|--------|--------|
| T−60 | Usage + value report; risk flags |
| T−45 | Commercial proposal for next term |
| T−30 | Legal/procurement path confirm |
| T−14 | Decision meeting |
| T−0 | Renewed or churned with reason code |

**Renewal principles:** Prefer multi-quarter pilots converting to annual only after proven adoption. Never hostage-renew on fear; renew on measured workflow value.

## Stage exit SLAs (internal)

| Transition | Target |
|------------|--------|
| Lead → Qualify decision | ≤3 business days |
| Qualify → Discovery complete | ≤10 business days |
| Discovery → Demo | ≤7 business days |
| Demo → Proposal | ≤5 business days |
| Proposal → Won/Lost | ≤30 days for pilot-scope deals (flag if longer) |

Stuck deals >45 days without next step → move to Nurture or Closed-Lost.

---

## Loss reason codes

- No budget / priority shift  
- Chose competitor  
- Security/compliance gap (honest)  
- MoR/checkout friction  
- Unauthorized-use mismatch (disqualify)  
- No champion  
- Timing / project cancelled  
- Price / packaging
