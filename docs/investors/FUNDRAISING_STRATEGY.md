## 1. Strategic principle

**Raise to accelerate a working commercial engine—not to paper over a blocked checkout.**

Until Paddle MoR collection is reliable, prioritize operator capital, organic GTM, and design-partner learning. Capital markets are optional; independence is a valid path.

---

## 2. Path A — Bootstrap / operator-funded

### When it fits

- MoR still activating or newly live  
- Founder bandwidth can cover support + light outbound  
- Goal is proof of paid conversion, not vanity growth  

### What to fund with bootstrap cash

1. GPU wallet for generation reliability (Fal)  
2. Domain / email / MoR operational fees  
3. Minimal paid experiments only after checkout works  
4. Legal counsel for contracts as needed  

### Success criteria (operational — VERIFIED goals, not invented metrics)

- Checkout completes for Tester / Standard / Premium  
- Support@ responds within published expectations  
- AUP/Identity Preservation messaging stays clean  
- First paid accounts exist as **facts** before storytelling scale  

### Pros / cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| No dilution | Slower brand build |
| Discipline on CAC | Harder to hire ahead of revenue |
| Credible stage narrative | Competitive category is capital-rich |

## 3. Path B — Angel / pre-seed exploration

### Gate checklist (must be true as FACTS before serious outreach)

| Gate | Why |
|------|-----|
| MoR checkout path production-stable | Investors will test buy |
| Legal v1.1 live (already VERIFIED) | Diligence hygiene |
| Product demo reliable | Engineering freeze helps |
| Early paid retention signal | Even small cohorts beat fake charts |
| Honest data room | This Sprint 5 pack |

### Target investor profile (ASSUMPTION)

- Angels who understand SaaS + creative tools  
- Operators from marketing / media / developer tools  
- Pakistan / South Asia / diaspora angels comfortable with global SaaS distribution  
- Avoid investors who require unauthorized deepfake positioning  

### Typical use of angel capital (ASSUMPTION)

See [`USE_OF_FUNDS.md`](./USE_OF_FUNDS.md) — prioritize GPU buffer, CS hire, agency outbound, conversion optimization.

### Process

1. Update MoR status one-pager weekly  
2. Circulate memorandum + deck content (not fake metrics)  
3. 20–30 qualified intros via warm network (see Investor Outreach Strategy)  
4. Counsel on SAFE/equity before signing  

---

## 4. Path C — Seed

### When it makes sense (ASSUMPTION)

- Agency playbook repeatable (pipeline → paid → expand)  
- Measured CAC/LTV directional (even if early)  
- Quality competitive for price point in ICP  
- Clear product roadmap that capital unlocks (not “buy users”)  

### When it does not

- To invent growth before checkout works  
- To fund unlimited GPU without utilization discipline  
- To claim enterprise readiness without pilot evidence  

### Narrative at seed

“Trust-first AI video studio with transparent seconds economics, marketing-operator DNA, and early agency motion—raising to scale GTM and reliability, not to discover product-market fit from zero.”

Illustrative roadmap bands remain PLANNING ASSUMPTION in Business Roadmap—not seed commitments.

## 5. Path D — Strategic / corporate partnerships (non-dilutive or hybrid)

### Partnership types

| Type | Value to RTAS | Value to partner | Dilution |
|------|---------------|------------------|----------|
| Agency reseller / referral | Distribution | Capacity for clients | None / revenue share |
| Creative tool integration | Workflow embed | Differentiated AI video | Possible rev share |
| Cloud / GPU credits | COGS relief | Usage commitment | None |
| Media / brand design partner | Case study (real only) | Early access | None |
| Corporate venture | Capital + channel | Option on category | Dilutive if equity |

### Rules

- No fake co-marketing logos  
- AUP alignment mandatory  
- Prefer partnerships that survive without inventing exclusivity claims  
- Document commercial terms; counsel for exclusivity  

Detail: GTM partnerships section in [`docs/business/sales/Go-To-Market-Strategy.md`](../business/sales/Go-To-Market-Strategy.md) · acquisition outreach in Sprint 4 pack.

## 6. Path E — Later growth / Series A exploration

**Only if** efficient growth + retention + enterprise wedge evidence exist. Otherwise remain profitable-lean / operator-funded. Series A is a **choice**, not a destiny (aligned with Business Roadmap funding logic).

## 9. Communication rules (non-negotiable)

1. Never invent MRR, logos, or “committed term sheets.”  
2. Always disclose Paddle activation status accurately.  
3. Label TAM/SAM/SOM and roadmap revenue as estimates/assumptions.  
4. Refuse positioning that breaks Paddle AUP / Trust & Safety.  
5. Keep legal and engineering version claims current (v1.1 / v1.0.0).
