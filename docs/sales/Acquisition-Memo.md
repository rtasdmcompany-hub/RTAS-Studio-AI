## 1. Why acquire — technology

**Verified assets:**

- Integrated generation product loop (Studio UX → credit guards → job queue → Fal render → library entitlements)  
- Shared packages for credits, categories, legal content, and billing semantics  
- Modern stack: Next.js, FastAPI, Prisma/Supabase, Vercel, Cloudflare DNS, Resend  

**Acquirer value:** Compresses years of “assemble a video SaaS” engineering into an operable codebase with documented architecture and security baselines—especially valuable if the buyer has distribution but lacks a finished creator/agency studio surface.

## 8. Estimated acquisition reasoning (frameworks only)

Use standard software M&A frameworks. **Do not treat example numbers as offers.**

### 8.1 Strategic premium checklist

| Factor | Diligence question |
|--------|--------------------|
| Product completeness | Can buyers generate and pay end-to-end today? |
| MoR status | Is Paddle live checkout fully activated? |
| COGS | What is GPU cost per second at quality targets? |
| Compliance | Any residual AUP / likeness risk? |
| Key person | Continuity of operator and engineering ownership? |
| IP | License boundaries of third-party models vs. owned UX/IP? |

### 8.2 Valuation frameworks (Estimate language only)

| Framework | How it is used | Caution |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **Revenue multiple** | EV ≈ ARR × sector multiple | Requires **verified** ARR; none invented here |
| **Gross-profit multiple** | Better when GPU COGS dominate | Needs audited COGS |
| **DCF** | NPV of projected FCF | Label all projections **illustrative** |
| **Replacement cost** | Cost to rebuild Studio + billing + compliance | Useful floor for acquirers with distribution |
| **Strategic synergy** | Distribution × attach rate uplift | Must be buyer-specific |

### 8.3 Illustrative stage bands (not a valuation)

| Stage (descriptive) | Typical private SaaS diligence posture | Application to RTAS |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------|---------------------|
| Pre-revenue / activation-gated | Heavier weight on tech + team + strategic fit | MoR may still be pending — honest |
| Early revenue | Trailing ARR + growth + NRR | Only after verified billing data |
| Scale | Rule of 40, NRR, net retention cohorts | Future state |

**Any numeric “valuation” in a live deal must come from negotiated diligence—not this memo.**

## 10. Risks an acquirer will price

- MoR / checkout activation incomplete  
- GPU margin volatility  
- Model-provider dependency (Fal and peers)  
- Category competition from Runway, Pika, Kling, Luma, HeyGen, Synthesia, Veo, OpenAI surfaces  
- Policy risk if marketing ever drifts from authorized-content positioning  

Mitigations already present: legal v1.1, Trust & Safety, AI Policy, credit clarity, docs pack.

---

## 11. Recommendation (strategic, not financial advice)

RTAS Studio AI is most compelling as a **productized AI video SaaS with compliance-aware positioning and MoR-native commercial design**. Acquisition logic should emphasize **time-to-product**, **payments/compliance readiness**, and **distribution synergy**—and should wait for **verified financials** before applying revenue multiples.
