## 2. Universal fit screen (all categories)

An ideal acquirer—regardless of sector—typically needs:

| Criterion | Why it matters for RTAS |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Distribution to creators, agencies, or brand marketers | RTAS packaging is studio + commercial workflow |
| Willingness to buy **systems + UX + trust**, not only model weights | Foundation models are licensed (e.g. Fal path)—not RTAS-owned |
| Comfort with early-stage traction honesty | No fabricated ARR in materials |
| Respect for authorized-content / AUP posture | Legal v1.1 and Trust & Safety are part of the asset |
| Ability to complete build-vs-buy math | Replacement-cost lens in Sprint 3 valuation memo |

**Disqualifiers (poor cultural fit):** Demands unauthorized deepfake positioning; requires invented metrics; expects owned frontier-model IP; refuses MoR / payments compliance discipline.

## 3. Category profiles

### Profile A — Generative video / creative AI platforms

**Who they are:** Companies whose core product is AI video or generative creative tooling.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Accelerate “commercial studio” packaging (credits, tiers, library, agency workflows) and a compliance-aware Identity Preservation narrative without rebuilding SaaS glue.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Differentiation vs their internal roadmap  
- Overlap risk (feature absorption vs standalone brand)  
- GPU / provider contracts and COGS per second  
- MoR checkout reliability  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** Clean product loop; credit economics clarity; documented architecture; honest stage.

**Poor fit signals:** Buyer only wants model research talent; no interest in SaaS packaging.

**RTAS proof package:** Live demo · architecture docs · pricing book · legal v1.1 · tech DD memo.

---

### Profile B — Avatar / enterprise presenter-video vendors expanding into campaign creative

**Who they are:** Talking-head, training, and avatar-first video SaaS seeking cinematic / marketing creative breadth.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Fill a portfolio gap (commercials, music-video-style, animation-oriented generation) while reusing their enterprise sales motion.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Integration into existing workspaces / SSO expectations  
- Enterprise security questionnaire depth  
- Brand-safety and likeness controls  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** Strong Trust & Safety / AI Policy; clear authorized-use framing; Standard/Premium packaging.

**Poor fit:** Buyer requires on-prem GPU ownership RTAS does not provide.

**RTAS proof package:** Legal suite · security docs · enterprise sales process (Sprint 4) · known limitations honesty.

---

### Profile C — Martech, ad-creative, and campaign platforms

**Who they are:** Marketing clouds, social suites, performance creative tools, DAM-adjacent platforms needing generation inside workflows.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Attach generative video to existing seats and campaign budgets; shorten time-to-module vs greenfield build.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- API / embed path maturity (today: product-first; partner API is expansion option)  
- Multi-seat / agency packaging (Premium/Agency SKUs may be proposed, not all live)  
- Customer success capacity  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** ICP oriented to agencies and marketing companies; credit metering maps to campaign cost control.

**Poor fit:** Buyer needs deep native integrations yesterday with no transition plan.

**RTAS proof package:** ICP · GTM · pricing · partnership framework · growth docs (Sprint 8).

---

### Profile D — DAM / MAM / media toolchain vendors

**Who they are:** Digital asset management, media asset management, and post-production toolchain companies.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Add generative create → store → distribute loops adjacent to asset libraries.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Asset ownership / customer content handling under Privacy & Terms  
- Library entitlements and export controls  
- Rights metadata discipline  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** Library + entitlements design; privacy posture; MoR refund clarity.

**Poor fit:** Buyer assumes RTAS holds exclusive content licenses it does not.

---

### Profile E — AI infrastructure / GPU platforms seeking vertical apps

**Who they are:** Inference platforms, GPU marketplaces, and infra companies wanting application-layer attach.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Showcase vertical SaaS demand on their compute; own a reference “studio” SKU.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Provider lock-in vs multi-provider routing readiness  
- Gross margin sensitivity to GPU price moves  
- Whether RTAS can remain multi-cloud friendly post-deal  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** FastAPI worker gateway and credit metering as COGS observability surface.

**Poor fit:** Buyer insists RTAS abandon compliance packaging.

---

### Profile F — Agency networks and holding-company digital groups

**Who they are:** Networks that productize production capacity for member agencies.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Internalize AI production tooling; leverage RTAS Digital Marketing Company adjacency for services-led distribution.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Services vs software boundary  
- Geographic operating base (Pakistan) and client confidentiality  
- Utilization economics across many agencies  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** Agency ICP; operator marketing DNA; clear SaaS-first model.

**Poor fit:** Buyer wants a pure services studio rebrand, killing product roadmap.

---

### Profile G — Regional technology / media groups (South Asia, MENA, diaspora-aware)

**Who they are:** Regional digital groups expanding English-first global SaaS footprints.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Acquire a finished global product surface with local operator knowledge and lower rebuild cost.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Cross-border entity / IP assignment cleanliness  
- MoR global payout reliability  
- Brand continuity under RTAS ecosystem  

**Ideal signals from RTAS:** Live international domain; English GTM; Pakistan operating base stated consistently.

**Poor fit:** Buyer cannot handle international MoR / tax mechanics.

---

### Profile H — Financial buyers (growth equity / PE) — later-stage only

**Who they are:** Funds seeking software cash-flow or growth assets.

**Why they might buy (thesis — ASSUMPTION):** Only after **verified** ARR, retention, and margin—not at documentation-only stage.

**What they diligence hardest:**

- Audited or diligence-grade financials  
- Cohort retention / NRR  
- Sales efficiency  

**Current RTAS posture:** **Not primary** until MoR collections and metrics exist. See Sprint 3 exit strategy and Sprint 5 fundraising gates.

**Poor fit today:** Any process that requires fabricated historicals.
