## 3. Scenario A — Stabilize (conservative)

**Theme:** Make commerce real; avoid premature scale spend.

### Assumptions (PLANNING)

- MoR activates slowly or needs repeated remediation.  
- GTM is founder-led outbound + organic content.  
- Paid ads remain off until checkout conversion is proven.  
- No external raise required.

### Illustrative outcomes (ASSUMPTION — not forecast)

| Horizon | Narrative band |
|---------|----------------|
| 12 months | Low hundreds of paying accounts or fewer; revenue near roadmap **Low** band |
| 24 months | Modest Standard-heavy base; Premium niche |
| 36 months | Sustainable small SaaS or services-assisted hybrid |

### Investment / acquisition implication

- Investment: bootstrap default.  
- Acquisition: build-vs-buy conversations only; financial buyers unlikely.

### Success criteria (operational)

- Checkout works.  
- Support answers.  
- Zero AUP marketing drift.  
- First reconciled MoR month exists as a **fact**.

## 4. Scenario B — Efficient climb (base)

**Theme:** Self-serve ladder works; light agency motion.

### Assumptions (PLANNING)

- MoR stable within planning horizon.  
- Tester → Standard conversion becomes measurable.  
- Content/SEO and founder outbound compound.  
- Optional angel capital only after early paid retention signal.

### Illustrative outcomes (ASSUMPTION)

| Horizon | Narrative band |
|---------|----------------|
| 12 months | Align toward business roadmap **Base** illustrative revenue band |
| 24 months | Agency accounts become meaningful mix |
| 36 months | Recognized niche commercial AI video studio option |

### Investment / acquisition implication

- Investment: conditional angel / pre-seed per Sprint 5 gates.  
- Acquisition: strategic categories A/C/F become more credible with verified ARR.

## 5. Scenario C — Agency scale

**Theme:** Marketing companies and agencies drive land-and-expand.

### Assumptions (PLANNING)

- Multi-seat / preferred agency packaging introduced when product supports (some SKUs may still be **proposed**).  
- Design partners convert to paid Standard/Premium.  
- Customer success motion exists (even lean).  
- Seed exploration only if retention and sales efficiency evidence exists.

### Illustrative outcomes (ASSUMPTION)

| Horizon | Narrative band |
|---------|----------------|
| 12–24 months | Toward roadmap **High** agency-weighted path if execution holds |
| 36 months | Stronger strategic premium thesis in valuation frameworks |

### Investment / acquisition implication

- Investment: seed possible **if** metrics are real.  
- Acquisition: martech / agency-network profiles prioritize RTAS.

### Risks

- Support load; custom contract sprawl; discount governance failure.

---

## 6. Scenario D — Partnership-accelerated

**Theme:** Distribution via integrations, marketplaces, or preferred networks—not only direct CAC.

### Assumptions (PLANNING)

- Affiliate/referral program launches **only after** MoR + attribution ready (today: proposed).  
- 1–3 serious distribution partnerships (not invented; must be signed to count).  
- Co-marketing stays metrics-honest.  
- Strategic minority investment possible alongside alliance.

### Illustrative outcomes (ASSUMPTION)

| Horizon | Narrative |
|---------|-----------|
| Near term | Partner-sourced pipeline supplements outbound |
| Mid term | Attach rate inside partner ecosystems becomes diligence story |
| Long term | Partnership may mature into Profile C/D/E/F acquisition dialogue |

### Investment / acquisition implication

- Investment: strategic corporate capital more relevant than pure financial.  
- Acquisition: optionality rises **without** requiring hypergrowth vanity.

### Risks

- Channel conflict; exclusivity traps; partner AUP incidents.

---

## 7. Scenario E — Category push (aggressive)

**Theme:** Capitalized push for brand presence against well-funded generative video incumbents.

### Assumptions (PLANNING)

- External capital committed (amount **not** asserted here—see use-of-funds scenarios).  
- Paid acquisition scaled after MoR green.  
- Product depth (API, workspaces, enterprise controls) accelerates.  
- Competitive response expected.

### Illustrative outcomes (ASSUMPTION)

| Horizon | Narrative |
|---------|-----------|
| 12–24 months | Faster top-of-funnel; higher burn; Rule-of-40 pressure |
| 36 months | Either breakout niche leadership **or** consolidation (sale/merger) |

### Investment / acquisition implication

- Investment: required for this path.  
- Acquisition: more likely as either upside exit or defensive consolidation—**neither promised**.

### Risks

- Capital inefficiency; narrative overclaim; MoR/compliance stress under growth.
